Chickadilla Soup

History and Notes on Chickadilla Soup

Chickadilla soup is also known as Tortilla Soup and has roots in Spain and Mexico from the 16th century. Indigenous people in Mexico would consume corn tortillas, tomatoes, and chillies as a main part of their diet. The Spanish then introduced Chicken and Cheese which was brought to the US in the 16th century. The Aztecs also had a similar soup that had chicken broth, roasted tomatoes, onions, garlic, and chilies. The name doesn't have historical roots and is likely from a more modern-American combination of Chicken Tortilla and Chicken enchilada soup.

Sources: eatzocalo.com, thecatrinashop.com

Chickadilla soup is a recipe my family has been cooking for a very long time, and I remember having it when I was younger. It's a very fufilling meal on it's own but can also be served with tortilla chips, avocado, or oyster crackers. This soup is really good for freezing, even for several months, and eating later in the year. My family usually eats it around the start of the fall season.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound of boneless skinless chicken things
  • 30 ounces of diced tomatoes
  • 10 ounces of mild enchilada sauce
  • 4 ounces of chopped mild green chilies
  • 1 quart of chicken broth
  • 30 ounces of black or pino beans (or a mix)
  • 10 ounces of frozen corn
  • 1/2 of a medium onion, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon of fresh cilantro
  • 1 teaspoon (each) of cumin, chili powder, and salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper

Additional serving options: tortilla chips, oyster crackers,
shredded cheest, sour cream, hot sauce, lime, avocado

Directions

  1. Place all ingredients into a slow cooker ("crock pot") on low for 6-8 hours or high for 3-4 hours.
  2. About halfway through cooking, once the chicken is cooked through, remove it from the pot, shred it, and return it for the remaining time.
  3. Let cool brifely before serving in bowls with serving options listed above.
